# POL-005-SENIOR-VULNERABLE-CLIENTS — SENIOR AND VULNERABLE CLIENT REVIEWS

Document Owner: Compliance Operations
Version: 3.0
Last Updated: June 2026
Internal Distribution: Advisory, Compliance, Branch Managers, Audit

---

# 1. Purpose

This policy establishes supervisory expectations for recommendations involving:

* senior clients,
* retired clients,
* vulnerable clients,
* cognitively impaired clients,
* and clients experiencing diminished decision-making capacity.

The primary objective of this policy is to ensure recommendations remain appropriate where age, health, dependency, or vulnerability factors may materially increase client risk.

This policy supplements, but does not replace, standard suitability requirements.

---

# 2. Senior Client Considerations

Senior clients may experience:

* reduced employment income,
* increased reliance on retirement savings,
* greater liquidity needs,
* shorter recovery periods following investment losses,
* and increased sensitivity to market volatility.

Supervisory review should consider whether recommendations expose senior clients to risks that may be difficult to recover from financially.

---

# 3. Vulnerability Indicators

Potential indicators of vulnerability include:

* age above 65,
* cognitive decline,
* memory concerns,
* diminished financial literacy,
* reliance on family members or caregivers,
* significant health issues,
* bereavement,
* isolation,
* or recent major life events.

The existence of a vulnerability indicator does not imply incapacity.

However, it may require enhanced review and documentation.

---

# 4. Liquidity Requirements

Recommendations involving the following require additional scrutiny for senior or vulnerable clients:

* illiquid investments,
* long lock-up periods,
* deferred redemption schedules,
* private placements,
* speculative products,
* or highly volatile securities.

Supervisors should evaluate whether the client may reasonably require access to invested funds.

---

# 5. Client Understanding

Advisors should take reasonable steps to confirm that vulnerable clients understand:

* the nature of the investment,
* potential losses,
* liquidity limitations,
* and key product risks.

Evidence of understanding should be documented where elevated risk exists.

---

# 6. Escalation Triggers

The following situations generally require supervisory review:

* senior client speculative trading,
* elderly client high-risk product purchases,
* significant concentration exposure involving retired clients,
* signs of diminished understanding,
* concerns regarding third-party influence,
* unusual withdrawals followed by speculative purchases,
* or possible financial exploitation.

---

# 7. Documentation Expectations

Advisor notes should explain:

* why the recommendation remains appropriate,
* client liquidity considerations,
* discussions regarding risks,
* and evidence supporting client understanding.

---

# 8. Supervisory Review Notes

The purpose of this policy is to protect senior and vulnerable clients from unsuitable recommendations and elevated financial harm.

Reviews should focus on vulnerability, client capacity, liquidity needs, and retirement-related considerations.
