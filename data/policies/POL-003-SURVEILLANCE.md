# POL-003-SURVEILLANCE — TRADE SURVEILLANCE AND ESCALATION PROCEDURES

Department: Compliance Operations
Version: 1.8
Confidential Internal Use Only

---

# 1. Overview

The Trade Surveillance Program exists to identify:

* suitability concerns,
* supervisory gaps,
* documentation deficiencies,
* and elevated conduct risk indicators.

Trade alerts generated through automated monitoring systems are reviewed using a risk-based triage framework.

Automated alerts do not independently determine rule violations.

Human supervisory judgment remains required.

---

# 2. Workflow Routing Categories

Trade alerts may be routed into one of the following supervisory queues:

## AUTO-PASS

Cases with:

* low estimated suitability risk,
* complete documentation,
* low concentration exposure,
* and no elevated supervisory indicators
  may be automatically cleared through straight-through processing.

Auto-pass designation does not imply guaranteed compliance.

Auto-passed cases remain subject to:

* supervisory sampling,
* retrospective review,
* and audit examination.

Minor documentation imperfections or soft contextual indicators may still be auto-passed where estimated suitability risk is low, KYC is complete, and no material concern signal is present.

---

## QUEUE REVIEW

Queue review applies where a trade appears potentially compliant but requires human confirmation due to:

* moderate risk indicators,
* uncertain KYC,
* incomplete documentation that limits suitability confirmation,
* overexposure concerns,
* or soft supervisory indicators.

---

## PRIORITY REVIEW

Priority review applies where hard compliance violations or significant suitability concerns are present,including:

* missing KYC,
* risk tolerance mismatch,
* experience-complexity mismatch,
* elevated concentration exposure,
* or multiple cumulative concern signals.

Advisor conduct history may increase review priority when combined with current trade-level concerns, repeated alerts, or documentation weaknesses. Advisor history alone should not determine priority routing.

Priority items should generally be reviewed before standard queue items.

---

## URGENT ESCALATION

Urgent review applies where severe or compounding risk indicators suggest elevated potential client harm, such as:

* multiple hard suitability violations,
* missing KYC combined with high-risk or unsuitable products,
* elderly or vulnerable client exposure to speculative or complex products,
* materially deficient documentation combined with elevated client harm risk,
* or severe concentration / liquidity risk.

No single indicator independently requires urgent escalation unless the risk of client harm is clearly elevated.

Urgent cases may require:

* immediate supervisory review,
* trade cancellation or reversal consideration, where permitted,
* or temporary account restrictions.

---

# 3. Surveillance Risk Indicators

The surveillance framework evaluates:

* concentration exposure,
* product volatility,
* advisor conduct history,
* rationale completeness,
* KYC consistency,
* client vulnerability,
* and historical supervisory outcomes.

Risk indicators are cumulative and may elevate workflow routing outcomes.

No single indicator independently determines final escalation level.

---

# 4. Missing Documentation

Missing rationale documentation is considered a supervisory weakness.

Trades lacking supporting rationale may be escalated where the missing documentation prevents reviewers from confirming suitability, or where other risk indicators are present.

Minor documentation weaknesses may reduce confidence without independently requiring escalation.

Supervisors should determine whether:

* suitability analysis occurred,
* required disclosures were made,
* and documentation standards were followed.

---

# 5. Advisor Conduct Indicators

Supervisory review may consider:

* prior compliance events,
* repeated suitability alerts,
* excessive KYC amendments,
* repeated speculative trading patterns,
* or elevated complaint history.

Historical conduct indicators should not independently determine supervisory conclusions but may increase review priority.

---

# 6. Review Outcomes

Supervisory review outcomes may include:

* approved,
* rejected,
* escalated,
* or pending further investigation.

Escalated reviews may require:

* branch management review,
* compliance consultation,
* or enhanced client contact procedures.

---

# 7. Audit Expectations

All review actions must be logged with:

* timestamps,
* reviewer identifiers,
* rationale summaries,
* and supervisory outcomes.

Review notes should explain:

* why the trade was approved,
* rejected,
* or escalated.

Generic review comments are discouraged.

---

# 8. Model-Assisted Review Guidance

Automated review outputs are supervisory aids only.

Reviewers must independently assess:

* retrieved evidence,
* client suitability,
* and advisor rationale.

Model outputs should not replace supervisory judgment.

Compliance staff remain accountable for final review decisions.
