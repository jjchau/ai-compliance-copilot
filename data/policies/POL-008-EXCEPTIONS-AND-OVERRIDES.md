# POL-008-EXCEPTIONS-AND-OVERRIDES — EXCEPTION HANDLING AND POLICY OVERRIDES

Document Owner: Compliance Operations
Version: 2.5
Last Updated: March 2025
Internal Distribution: Advisory, Compliance, Branch Managers, Audit

---

# 1. Purpose

This policy establishes supervisory expectations regarding policy exceptions and override decisions.

Not all transactions can be evaluated through standard suitability criteria.

Certain client circumstances may justify recommendations that deviate from normal supervisory expectations.

Such situations may require enhanced review and documentation where the exception materially affects suitability, risk exposure, client understanding, or supervisory confidence.

---

# 2. General Principles

Policy exceptions are intended to address unusual circumstances and should not be used to routinely bypass supervisory controls.

The existence of a policy exception does not eliminate suitability obligations.

Advisors remain responsible for demonstrating that recommendations remain appropriate for the client.

Supervisors should assess both:

* the reason for the exception,
* and the risks created by the exception.

---

# 3. Examples of Potential Exceptions

Examples may include:

* legacy concentrated holdings,
* inherited positions,
* employer stock ownership,
* specialized investment expertise,
* unusual liquidity events,
* client-directed speculative activity,
* or unique tax planning considerations.

The presence of an exception does not automatically justify approval.

---

# 4. Documentation Requirements

Advisor rationale should explain:

* why the exception exists,
* why standard suitability guidance may not fully apply,
* risks discussed with the client,
* and why the recommendation remains appropriate.

Documentation should specifically address any increased risk created by the exception.

Generic references to client preference may be insufficient.

---

# 5. Supervisory Review Expectations

Supervisors should evaluate:

* client objectives,
* investment knowledge,
* risk capacity,
* account composition,
* concentration exposure,
* and overall suitability.

Additional documentation may be required before approval.

Supervisors may reject recommendations where risks cannot be adequately justified.

---

# 6. Escalation Triggers

Additional review is generally required where:

* multiple exceptions exist simultaneously,
* speculative products are involved,
* significant concentration exists,
* elderly or vulnerable clients are affected,
* documentation is materially incomplete and prevents supervisors from understanding the basis for the exception,
* or previous exception requests have occurred repeatedly.

Escalation may include:

* branch manager review,
* compliance consultation,
* or enhanced supervisory approval.

---

# 7. Override Authority

Override authority should be exercised conservatively.

Approval of an override indicates that supervisory review has occurred and supporting documentation is considered adequate.

Overrides should not be granted solely to facilitate transaction completion.

Supervisors may impose additional documentation requirements before approval.

---

# 8. Supervisory Review Notes

Exceptions should remain infrequent and appropriately documented.

Repeated exception activity involving the same advisor, product category, or client relationship may warrant additional supervisory review.

Exception approval does not eliminate accountability for suitability outcomes.
