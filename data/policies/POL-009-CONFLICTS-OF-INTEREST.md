# POL-009-CONFLICTS-OF-INTEREST — CONFLICTS OF INTEREST GUIDANCE

Document Owner: Compliance Operations
Version: 2.1
Last Updated: February 2025
Internal Distribution: Advisory, Compliance, Branch Managers, Audit

---

# 1. Purpose

This policy establishes expectations regarding the identification, disclosure, and management of conflicts of interest.

Client interests must remain the primary consideration when providing investment recommendations.

Conflicts should be identified and addressed before recommendations are implemented.

---

# 2. General Principles

A conflict of interest exists where personal, financial, business, or institutional interests may influence professional judgment.

Conflicts may arise regardless of whether improper conduct occurs.

The existence of a conflict requires appropriate management.

Advisors should consider both actual and reasonably foreseeable conflicts.

---

# 3. Examples of Potential Conflicts

Examples may include:

* compensation incentives,
* referral arrangements,
* proprietary product recommendations,
* sales targets,
* outside business activities,
* personal financial interests,
* or relationships with issuers.

Not all conflicts are prohibited.

However, all material conflicts should be appropriately managed.

---

# 4. Client-First Considerations

Recommendations should be based primarily on client needs and circumstances.

Advisors should not recommend products solely because:

* compensation is higher,
* incentives are available,
* sales targets exist,
* or supervisory pressure is present.

Where multiple suitable options exist, advisors should consider the client's interests when making recommendations.

---

# 5. Disclosure Expectations

Material conflicts should be disclosed in a manner that is:

* clear,
* meaningful,
* timely,
* and understandable.

Disclosure alone may not be sufficient to manage certain conflicts.

Additional controls may be required.

---

# 6. Supervisory Review Expectations

Supervisors should consider whether recommendations may have been influenced by:

* compensation structures,
* product incentives,
* sales campaigns,
* concentration in proprietary products,
* or other potential conflicts.

Additional review may be warranted where conflict indicators are present.

---

# 7. Escalation Triggers

The following situations generally require supervisory review:

* unusually concentrated proprietary product exposure,
* repeated recommendations involving incentive programs,
* excessive switching activity,
* compensation-driven product patterns,
* unresolved conflict disclosures,
* or advisor conduct concerns.

Escalated reviews may require compliance consultation or enhanced documentation.

Conflict indicators should be evaluated together with suitability, documentation, advisor conduct history, and client impact. A potential conflict alone does not determine the final supervisory outcome where it is adequately disclosed, managed, and not linked to unsuitable recommendations.

---

# 8. Supervisory Review Notes

Conflict management is intended to support objective client-focused recommendations.

Supervisors should evaluate both the existence of conflicts and the effectiveness of mitigation measures.

Failure to appropriately identify or manage conflicts may constitute a supervisory concern even where suitability otherwise appears reasonable.
