# POL-010-CLIENT-OBJECTIVE — CLIENT OBJECTIVE ALIGNMENT

Document Owner: Retail Supervision
Version: 2.4
Last Updated: April 2025
Internal Distribution: Advisory, Compliance, Branch Managers, Audit

---

# 1. Purpose

This policy establishes supervisory expectations regarding alignment between investment recommendations and client objectives.

Investment objectives are a foundational component of suitability assessments.

Recommendations should reasonably support the objectives documented within the client's profile.

---

# 2. General Principles

Investment objectives should reflect the client's intended outcomes.

Common objectives include:

* capital preservation,
* income generation,
* balanced growth,
* long-term growth,
* and speculative growth.

Recommendations should be evaluated within the context of the client's stated objectives.

No objective should be considered in isolation from other suitability factors.

---

# 3. Capital Preservation Objectives

Clients with capital preservation objectives generally prioritize:

* stability,
* principal protection,
* liquidity,
* and reduced volatility.

Additional scrutiny may be required where recommendations involve:

* speculative products,
* concentrated positions,
* leveraged investments,
* illiquid products,
* or significant downside risk.

Recommendations inconsistent with preservation objectives require enhanced documentation.

---

# 4. Income Objectives

Income-oriented recommendations should consider:

* reliability of distributions,
* cash flow requirements,
* volatility,
* liquidity,
* and sustainability.

Advisors should evaluate whether income-producing investments expose clients to risks that may undermine income objectives.

---

# 5. Growth Objectives

Growth-oriented clients may reasonably accept greater volatility.

However, growth objectives do not automatically justify:

* excessive concentration,
* inappropriate leverage,
* speculative trading,
* or unsuitable product complexity.

Suitability considerations remain applicable regardless of growth orientation.

---

# 6. Objective Changes

Changes to investment objectives should be supported by:

* client discussions,
* updated KYC documentation,
* and appropriate rationale.

Objective changes immediately preceding high-risk recommendations may require supervisory review.

Frequent changes may indicate suitability concerns.

---

# 7. Documentation Expectations

Advisor rationale should explain:

* how the recommendation supports client objectives,
* risks discussed,
* expected benefits,
* and any material trade-offs.

Documentation should specifically address situations where recommendations appear inconsistent with documented objectives.

---

# 8. Escalation Triggers

Additional review is generally required where:

* capital preservation objectives coexist with speculative investments,
* income objectives coexist with significant volatility exposure,
* objective changes occur immediately before high-risk transactions,
* documentation is inconsistent,
* or suitability concerns remain unresolved.

Escalated cases may require:

* branch manager review,
* compliance consultation,
* enhanced documentation,
* or additional client confirmation.

---

# 9. Supervisory Review Notes

Client objectives are one component of suitability analysis.

Supervisors should evaluate objective alignment together with:

* risk tolerance,
* investment knowledge,
* time horizon,
* financial circumstances,
* and overall portfolio composition.

Recommendations should be assessed based on the totality of the client's circumstances rather than objective categories alone.
