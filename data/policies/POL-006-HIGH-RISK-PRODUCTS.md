# POL-006-HIGH-RISK-PRODUCTS — HIGH-RISK PRODUCT APPROVAL REQUIREMENTS

Document Owner: Product Supervision
Version: 2.3
Last Updated: February 2025
Internal Distribution: Advisory, Compliance, Branch Managers, Audit

---

# 1. Purpose

This policy establishes supervisory expectations for recommendations involving high-risk or complex investment products.

Certain products may expose clients to risks that exceed those associated with traditional diversified investments.

Enhanced suitability review may therefore be required.

---

# 2. Products Subject to Enhanced Review

Examples include:

* leveraged ETFs,
* inverse ETFs,
* options strategies,
* speculative mining issuers,
* cryptocurrency-related securities,
* private placements,
* exempt market products,
* illiquid investments,
* structured products,
* and other complex securities.

The list is not exhaustive.

Supervisors may designate additional products for enhanced review.

Conventional diversified products such as standard mutual funds, standard non-leveraged ETFs, investment-grade bonds, GICs, and T-Bills are not automatically subject to this policy solely because they involve ordinary market risk.

Such products may become relevant to this policy only where additional facts indicate leverage, inverse exposure, illiquidity, speculative exposure, unusual volatility, embedded complexity, or another elevated-risk feature.

---

# 3. Client Knowledge Requirements

Product complexity should align with client investment knowledge and experience.

Additional review may be required where limited investment experience is combined with product complexity or elevated product risk, including where:

* product structure is complex,
* leverage is involved,
* inverse exposure is involved,
* liquidity is limited,
* speculative exposure is material,
* or significant volatility is expected.

Limited investment experience alone should not cause a conventional diversified product to be treated as high-risk or complex.

---

# 4. Risk Tolerance Alignment

High-risk products should generally align with:

* higher risk tolerance,
* longer investment horizons,
* sufficient financial capacity,
* and documented understanding of potential losses.

Recommendations involving complex products should be supported by specific rationale.

---

# 5. Concentration Considerations

Supervisors should evaluate:

* total exposure to high-risk products,
* cumulative speculative exposure,
* issuer concentration,
* and portfolio diversification.

Multiple speculative positions may collectively increase suitability concerns even where individual positions appear reasonable.

---

# 6. Documentation Expectations

Advisor rationale should explain:

* why the product is appropriate,
* how risks were discussed,
* alternative investments considered,
* and how the recommendation supports client objectives.

Documentation should address:

* volatility,
* liquidity,
* downside risk,
* and product complexity.

Generic statements such as "high growth opportunity" may be insufficient.

Documentation expectations should be applied in proportion to product complexity and client risk. Generic rationale is more concerning where the product is complex, speculative, leveraged, illiquid, concentrated, or otherwise elevated-risk.

---

# 7. Escalation Triggers

The following situations typically require supervisory review:

* high-risk product + low investment knowledge,
* speculative product + capital preservation objective,
* leveraged product + short documentation,
* concentrated speculative exposure,
* inconsistent KYC information,
* elderly client high-risk trading,
* or repeated product-related suitability alerts.

---

# 8. Supervisory Review Notes

Complex products require enhanced suitability assessment.

Approval should be based on the totality of client circumstances rather than product eligibility alone.

The existence of product disclosure documentation does not independently establish suitability.
